UCI Rocket Project

The UCI Rocket project is a team of undergraduate students that is working to break the undergraduate launch record for methalox rockets. After joining the team my sophomore year and successfully launching UCI's first ever methalox rocket, I have become the Chief Engineer for the team.

Before I became Chief Engineer, I was the Lead Avionics Engineer for the avionics team. The avionics team is responsible for researching, developing, and testing the electronic systems and software needed to launch and recover the rocket.

Ground Station GUI

After launch, our team immediately began researching and developing our next methalox rocket, MOCH4. Since then, I have been creating our new ground station and control center utilizing web-based technology. The control station uses a React frontend and Flask web service to monitor and control the state of the rocket, as well as run analysis on data we gather during our tests.

Engine Control Unit

The Engine Control Unit(ECU) was designed to be the brain of our rocket. It used a Teensy microcontroller running Arduino(C++) to control valve states and gather temperature and pressure readings on the rocket. I developed the code the ECU used to interpret signals from our ground station and translate them into actuating valves on our rocket.

Avionics Flight Sensors

The Avionics Flight System(AFS) is responsible for radio communication during flight and transmitting telemetry data to the ground station. I developed software AFS used to integrate with the ECU and retrieve solenoid valve states, tank pressures, and tank temperatures using I2C.

CLI

I also developed our CLI to allow us to test the rocket without setting up our ground station equipment. The CLI is written in python and uses an internet router connected to the rocket and an open wifi network to allow any laptop to connect to the rocket over wifi. This way, we could actuate valves on the rocket without the need for the switch box.
